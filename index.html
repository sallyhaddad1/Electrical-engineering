Power Demand Calculation

Each local load may be classified into several different categories for example, vital, Emergency and Normal.

All of the vital, essential and non-essential loads can be divided into typically three duty categories:
- Continuous operating (C)
- Intermittent operating (I)
- Standby (S)

The total amount power demand OF the project shall be the sum of C, I and S with suitable demand factor . The demand factor for continuous shall be 1 and for Intermittent shall be 0.30 and for standby shall be 0.10. The factors should be chosen in such a manner that the selection of Transformers/ generators and main feeders from a power utility company are not excessively rated, thereby leading to more equipment cost and poor operating efficiency.

Each of these totals will consist of the active power and the corresponding reactive power. Apparent power will be calculated from the active and reactive power component.

Once the maximum demand is calculated using the appropriate factors for C, I and S loads , the following margins to apply to get the Design capacity of power system
- Design margin of 10 %
- Spare future capacity of 10%

Hence for a total demand of 1000KVA , 100 KVA as design margin and 100 KVA as spare capacity the design capacity as 1200 KVA.

BASIC ELECTRICAL DISTRIBUTION – KEY SLD

BASIC ELECTRICAL DISTRIBUTION

Mainly 3 types of power distribution scheme – 0% redundant , 50 % redundant and 100% redundant . 100% redundant system is most preferable distribution scheme for industrial plant which requires high reliability. Two(2) 100% rated system will work at 50% loading during normal operation

KEY POINTS :
1) Basic Electrical Distribution is developed based on the Electrical Distribution philosophy defined in the scope( defining power boundary ) or electrical design basis document ( voltage levels to be maintained depending on power magnitude)
2) Key SLD or Overall SLD is developed indicating the entire power distribution of the plant along with the Substations and locations of each substation and panels. This is overall power plan.
3) Area wise SLD will be developed after the Equipment Sizing( defining individual S/S transformer sizing and no. of feeders necessary to cater that area load)
4) Key SLD or Overall SLD is developed indicating the :
- Power tapping arrangement from Plant Incomer
- Protection & Metering requirements for distribution system
- Number of Incomers in a switchboard bus and redundancy
- Power distribution at HV side and LV side
- HV & LV Voltage levels
- Bus bar material ( Al or Cu. Can be kept open for vendor to choose even based on current rating) and Short circuit withstand capacities & withstand times are indicated based on Design Philosophy document or Design Basis document (System studies will help validate and design the final Short circuit withstand ratings & times).
- Type of protection & starters to be considered for Motor Feeders (Both LV & HV level)
- Type of protection to be considered for Power Feeders (Both LV & HV level)
- Bus Arrangement ( single/split/double bus ) & Interlocks philosophy between buses( upstream /downstream or between bus sections)
- Emergency Power distribution philosophy
- Power Factor Compensation requirement

DETAILED ELECTRICAL DISTRIBUTION

Automatic Bus Transfer and Momentary paralleling scheme with 2 Incomer and 1 Bus Coupler switchgear


All the switchboards with sectionalised buses will be normally operated with the bus coupler breakers in open condition. Auto / Manual / Independent changeover between the two normal incomers and their bus-coupler will be provided with the following features through appropriate selector switch.

Auto Mode
For prolonged under voltage (due to tripping of primary side) on any one of the bus sections, the respective incoming breaker will trip and the bus coupler breaker will close provided the other bus section is healthy and bus coupler is selected for auto changeover. Total changeover time will be adjustable between 1 to 5 seconds. This auto changeover will be blocked if the incoming breaker had tripped on fault (on the load side). In case bus coupler trips on auto closing, no further closing shall be permitted till the system is reset. Changeover back to the normal source of supply will be affected manually.

Manual Mode
Manual live changeover facility will be provided for the switchboard.

For planned outage of one of the normal incoming supplies, the respective selected incomer will be tripped automatically after the bus section breaker is closed manually.

Also for restoration of normal power supply, the bus coupler will trip after the respective incoming breaker is closed.

For this purpose, a trip selector shall be provided for selecting the breaker to be tripped and momentary paralleling shall be done after phase comparison of both the power supplies through check synchronization relay.

Thus, depending on the selection made, the selected breaker will be tripped once all the three breakers are closed manually, thus maintaining continuity of supply for the complete switchboard. A timer with a time delay on pick up of 0.5 - 5 sec. for annunciation of the running breaker failing to trip within a pre-set time, (i.e. If the two sources remain paralleled for more than a pre-set time) will be provided.

DC BATTERY AND BATTERY CHARGER (DC UPS)

Purpose of Battery and battery Charger

The batteries are using for the control and protection of switchgears ( closing and tripping coils, spring charging motor, relays, indicating lamps) , DC lightings and any other DC application likes motors , solenoids etc. Battery chargers are using for charging the batteries and powering the DC loads during normal power supply

Battery :
 Reference Standard for sizing the battery capacity : IEEE 1115 for Nickel Cadmium Batteries and IEEE 485 for Lead Acid Batteries . The nominal voltage is NICD cell is 1.2 V and that Lead Acid is 2 V

Basic Terminologies :
 1- Duty Cycle : The duty cycle of a battery is defined as the load currents that a battery is expected to supply for specified periods of time.
2- End Cell Voltage : Cell Voltage allowed to fall before affecting the load. For Lead Acid Batteries it is 1.75V and for Nickel Cadmium it is 1.1 V.
3- Ampere Hour (AH) : The capacity of a secondary storage battery is usually expressed in amperes and time. For example, a battery capacity can be expressed as 42.5A for eight hours or 340 ampere-hours (Ah) at the eight hour rate ( However, the electrolyte temperature and final cell voltage also affects the AH rating of the battery.)
4- Capacity rating factor (Kt) : The capacity rating factor, Kt, is the ratio of rated ampere-hour capacity (at a standard time rate, at 25 °C, and to a standard end-of-discharge voltage) of a cell, to the amperes that can be supplied by that cell for t minutes at 25 °C and to a given end-of-discharge voltage.

Additional Criteria for Cell Sizing :
5- Temperature Correction Factor : The available capacity of a cell is affected by its operating temperature. The standard temperature for stating cell capacity is 25 °C. If the lowest expected electrolyte temperature is below standard, select a cell large enough to have the required capacity available at the lowest expected temperature. The battery manufacturer should be consulted for capacity derating factors for various discharge times and temperatures.
6- Design Margin : Required because of unforeseen additions to the dc system and less-than-optimum operating conditions of the battery due to improper maintenance, recent discharge, ambient temperatures lower than anticipated, or a combination of these factors. Design Margin is mentioned in Datasheet or Technical Specification, otherwise 10% is generally considered

AC UPS

Purpose :
- A UPS system is designed to provide a continuous, transient free supply of electrical power to critical loads to protect these loads from outages, surges, or dips in the normal AC power that is supplied from the utility or from an engine generator
- The output power that is provided by a UPS system is AC power, which is one of the key differences between UPS systems and DC systems

Main Components :
 - Rectifier : The Rectifier is a device that receives AC input power from the AC power source and that converts (rectifies) this AC input to a regulated DC output. The regulated DC output power is used for the following purposes:
a) To supply power to the inverter during normal system operation.
b) To maintain a float-charge on the UPS battery during normal system operation.
c) To recharge the UPS battery after a loss and the subsequent return of the normal AC power source.

- Battery : The function of the battery is to convert electrical energy into chemical energy during the charge cycle and to convert the stored chemical energy into electrical energy during the discharge cycle. After a loss of the normal AC power source, the battery will convert its stored chemical energy into electrical energy to supply the invertefr the required DC input so that the inverter can continue to supply the critical AC loads.

- Inverter : The function of the inverter of the UPS system is to convert the DC input from the battery charger or the battery to an AC sinewave output of the correct voltage and frequency to supply the critical AC loads.

- Static Switch : The function of the static switch is to automatically connect the critical AC loads to the output of the inverter during normal operation and, if the inverter output becomes unsatisfactory, to automatically transfer the critical AC loads from the inverter output to the bypass source.

- Bypass Source : The function of the bypass source is to provide an alternate source of AC power to the critical loads under the following conditions:
a) The inverter malfunctions and is unable to supply an output.
b) The current demand of the critical AC loads exceeds the capabilities of the inverter. Load in rush currents and faults are the typical causes of high current demands.

- Manual Bypass switch : The function of the manual bypass switch is to provide a means to manually transfer the critical AC loads from the inverter to the bypass source. This switch is normally used to keep the critical AC loads energized while maintenance is being performed on the inverter.

AC UPS

Types of UPS :

UPS systems are available with a variety of configurations and transfer schemes to meet the needs of each particular installation. The configuration of a UPS system is directly related to reliability and relative cost. Broadly it has been classified in to two types :
- Non-Redundant
- Redundant

Non-Redundant UPS :
 *** Simplest and least expensive
*** Least Reliable due to single path for power flow

Redundant UPS with Bypass :
 1- Parallel redundant and Hot standby system More reliable compared to Non Redundant without Bypass system due to addition of static switch and bypass source.
2- Static switch will transfer the critical AC loads to bypass source if the inverter output fails.
3- Costly compared to Non Redundant
4- The battery system can be 1x 100% or 2 x50 % .
5- In parallel redundant both UPS share load 50% and in hot stand by stand one system carries 100% load and other is work as standby

Operating Modes :
- Normal Mode
 1- AC load shall be continuously supplied by the UPS inverter.
2- Rectifier/charger shall derive power from a primary AC source and supply DC power to the inverter while simultaneously float-charging the battery.
3- Alternate power source will be energized.

- Backup Mode
 1- If failure of the primary AC power source occurs, the AC load shall continue to be supplied by the inverter, which shall obtain power from the battery source without any switching.
2- Power to the load shall not be interrupted upon failure or restoration of the primary AC power source.
3- After battery has discharged to minimum allowable voltage, the UPS shall transfer to the alternate AC power source if alternate source is available.

Recharge Mode :
1- Upon restoration of primary AC power source, the rectifier/charger shall recharge the batteries and shall simultaneously supply DC power to the inverter.
2- Recharge shall be an automatic function and shall not cause interruption to the load.

Bypass Mode :
 1- During an inverter malfunction, overcurrent condition, undervoltage or overvoltage condition, or load fault at the inverter output, the static switch shall automatically transfer the load to alternate AC power source with less than a 1/4-cycle break.
2- Upon clearing an inverter malfunction or fault condition, the static transfer switch shall automatically transfer the load from the alternate AC power source back to the inverter output, unless manual re-transfer mode has been selected. The re-transfer shall be without interruption (zero-break).

Metal-enclosed power switchgear: A switchgear assembly completely enclosed on all sides and top with sheet metal containing primary power circuit switching or interrupting devices, or both with buses ,connections, control and auxiliary devices. Access to the interior of the enclosure is provided by doors or removable covers, or both.

Metal-clad switchgear requires that the main switching and interrupting device be drawout. Circuit breakers are always electrically operated. Extensive barrier, shutters over the primary circuit elements when the interrupter is withdrawn, and insulation-covered bus are all required.

Insulators are mainly SMC (Sheet Moulding Compound)suitable up to 80KA and DMC (Dough Moulding Compound)Suitable up to 25KA

The specification shall indicate the devices(CB, MCCB, Contactors, relays, meters) specific requirement, busbar, fabrication details, instrument transformer, control and metering requirements, auxiliary busbar and earth busbar details, control wiring, incoming and outgoing connection details, control switches and indicating lamps specifics, painting and testing requirements .
